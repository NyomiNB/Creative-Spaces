Toledo Alliance for the Performing Arts

Dance - Toledo Ballet

Founded in 1939, the Toledo Ballet started off as an independent organization founded by dancer Marie Bollinger. Since 2019, the Toledo Ballet and Toledo Symphony have combined to create the Toledo Alliance for the Performing Arts, providing dance instruction for a variety of dance styles such as ballet, jazz, and modern dance. The Toledo Ballet also annually presents shows such as The Nutcracker and The Legend of Sleepy Hollow.

After reading more about the Toledo Ballet, are you interested in learning dance? No matter your age or skill level, the Toledo Ballet has a place for you! To learn more, email the Toledo Ballet at toledoballet@artstoledo.com.

Music - The Toledo Symphony and Toledo Jazz Orchestra

Founded in 1943 with around 20 musicians, the Toledo Symphony is comprised of 69 core players, and formed together with the Toledo Ballet (2019) and the Toledo Jazz Orchestra (2024) to create the Toledo Alliance for the Performing Arts, performing at venues such as the Stranahan Theater, the Peristyle, and also various places around the community to bring the joy of music throughout Northwest Ohio.

Start making music!

Ever had an interest in picking up a new hobby? Want a fun party trick? Or maybe you've fallen in love with the rich sound of the cello, the mellow sound of the viola, or the powerful sound of brass? From flute enthusiasts to violin fanatics, there's a place for you at the Toledo Symphony School of Music (TSSM)! It's never too late to become a musician--even if you're an adult, there's space for you at the school of music. If you have financial hardship don't let that deter you-scholarships are available!

Are you an elementary school student? Highschool student? Anything in between? If you've ever wanted to perform with friends, perform famous pieces like Beethoven's fifth, or more modern pieces like West Side Story? Or if you want the opportunity to work closely with Toledo Symphony Musicians, or participate in residency programs with famous musicians like Midori, then the Toledo Symphony Youth Orchestra is for YOU! With three varying difficulty levels for students of every skill level, the Toledo Symphony Youth Orchestra (TSYO) is an amazing opportunity for students to perform in a large group of people with common interests from all over Ohio and Southern Michigan.

Red Bird Sylvania

Visual Arts and Music - Art Galleries and Music Festivals

Red Bird Sylvania is an organization that coordinates local festivals such as Miracle on Main, a holiday event that features local food vendors, small boutiques, and cafes, as well as live music from local bands and school organizations.

Get involved in art or busking!

The Red Bird provides resources to find local galleries and art classes in the Sylvania area.

The Red Bird hosts "First Fridays," where the first Friday of each month has a fun-filled day of live music, local vendors, and more! If you're a musician, contact the Red Bird at redbirdartsdistrict@gmail.com or click the button below to read more about what First Fridays!

The Arts Commission of Greater Toledo

Visual Arts - Sculptures and Murals

Circa 1959, the Arts Commission of Greater Toledo advocates for the beautification of the Toledo area through murals and sculptures, as well as holding festivals such as the Toledo Trunk or Treat to promote local businesses and musicians!

Sylvania Arts

Visual Arts and Music- Film, Theater, Murals, and Music

Established in 1980, the Sylvania Art Commission started off giving back to the city of Sylvania through the Sylvania Community Orchestra and has since expanded to provide art, theater, film, and music programs for members of the community to enjoy.
